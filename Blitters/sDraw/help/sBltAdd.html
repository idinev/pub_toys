sBltAdd

sBltAdd performs an additive-blending transfer. Color is saturated.


void sBltAdd(long x,long y,long wid,long hei,long x2,long y2);

Parameters

x,y,wid,hei,x2,y2
The usual parameters

Return Value

None. [ASM: All registers are preserved.]

Example:

sBltAdd(0,0,400,300,0,0);

Remarks

sBltAdd disregards the alpha-channel of the source-sSprite.
sBltAdd is 8-10 times faster than sBltAlpha, and the visual result is often even better!
Additive blending is used in games as emulation of light (instead of having a dozen of similar textures with different light intensity).
An example usage in GUI is: making your interface's buttons glow.
